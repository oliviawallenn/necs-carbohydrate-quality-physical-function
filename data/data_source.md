# Data Source

## New England Centenarian Study (NECS)

The NECS is one of the largest studies of centenarians and their 
family members in the world, led by researchers at Boston University.
It collects longitudinal data on genetics, lifestyle, diet, and 
functional status in individuals who have survived to extreme old age.

- **Access:** Restricted. Data are available to researchers through 
  a formal data use agreement with the NECS team at Boston University.
  Contact: https://www.bumc.bu.edu/centenarian/
- **Analytic sample:** N = 438 participants after exclusion criteria
- **Follow-up:** Multiple visits; time variable = year of visit minus 
  year of enrollment

Raw data are not included in this repository per data use 
and confidentiality requirements.

## Datasets harmonized

| Dataset | Contents |
|---|---|
| FFQ | Food frequency questionnaire — all food items and frequencies |
| Nutrients | Nutrient-level values per food item |
| Cereal | Standardized cereal categories with GI/GL assignments |
| Demographics | Age, sex, education, chronic disease history, lifestyle |
| IADL | Instrumental Activities of Daily Living scores across visits |
